# Rewrite

Transform existing content by adjusting tone, length, style, or audience while preserving meaning.

## When to Use

- "Rewrite this to be more..."
- "Make this shorter/longer"
- "Change the tone to..."
- "Rephrase for [audience]"
- "Clean this up"
- "Make it more direct/warm/professional"

## Process

### 1. Get Content and Goal

- What text needs rewriting?
- What transformation is needed?
- Who is the audience?
- If no specific voice requested, default to Shintaro's voice (see **persona** skill)

### 2. Identify Transformation

| Type | What Changes |
|------|--------------|
| Tone | Formal to casual, warm to direct |
| Length | Expand or condense |
| Clarity | Simplify or add detail |
| Audience | Technical to general, exec to IC |
| Format | Prose to bullets, email to doc |
| Voice | Match a specific person's style |

### 3. Apply Transformation

**Keep:**
- Core meaning and facts
- Key arguments and structure
- Critical details

**Change:**
- Word choice (formal vs casual)
- Sentence length and complexity
- Level of detail
- Formatting and structure

### 4. Common Transformations

**Make it shorter:**
- Remove redundancy
- Cut qualifiers ("very", "really", "just")
- Combine sentences
- Delete obvious statements

**Make it more direct:**
- Active voice
- Short sentences
- State the point first
- Cut hedging ("I think", "It seems like")

**Make it warmer:**
- Add personal touches
- Acknowledge the reader
- Soften directives
- Use Shintaro's warm-but-direct style

**Make it clearer:**
- Simple words over complex
- Concrete examples over abstractions
- One idea per sentence

### 5. Present Result

Show the rewritten version. Offer: "Want me to adjust further?"

## Shintaro's Voice (Default)

When rewriting in Shintaro's voice, reference the **persona** skill and apply:
- Direct and warm, never stiff or corporate
- No em dashes
- No corporate jargon or passive voice
- Short paragraphs, white space
- Confident but not arrogant

## Anti-Patterns

- Don't change meaning while changing style
- Don't make it sound robotic or templated
- Don't lose critical information when shortening
- Don't over-transform. Subtle is often better
- Don't introduce AI-sounding language (see **de-ai** skill patterns)
