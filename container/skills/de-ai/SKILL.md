# De-AI

Strip AI writing patterns from text to make it sound authentically human.

## When to Use

- "De-ai this"
- "Remove AI patterns"
- "Make this sound human"
- "This sounds too AI"
- "Humanize this"
- After any AI-generated draft that needs to read naturally

## Process

### 1. Scan for AI Patterns

Read the text and flag every instance from the checklist below.

### 2. Rewrite

Replace flagged patterns with direct, specific, human alternatives. Principles:
- **Specific over generic** -- concrete details, not vague praise
- **Direct over hedged** -- state things confidently
- **Varied structure** -- don't default to lists and threes
- **Appropriate emphasis** -- match importance to actual significance

### 3. Present Result

Show the cleaned version, then list key changes:

```
Changes made:
- Removed [N] em dashes, replaced with commas
- Cut trailing "emphasizing the significance of" clause
- Changed "revolutionary, innovative, and groundbreaking" to "effective"
- Replaced "It's worth noting that X" with "X"
```

## AI Pattern Checklist

### Structural Patterns

**Rule of three overuse:**
- AI: "convenient, efficient, and innovative"
- Human: "convenient and efficient" (vary the count)

**Excessive em dashes:**
- AI uses em dashes where humans use commas, colons, or parentheses
- Replace most with commas or restructure the sentence

**Present participle trailing clauses:**
- AI: "...emphasizing the significance of..." / "...reflecting the continued relevance of..."
- Human: Cut these entirely. They add nothing.

**List-heavy structure:**
- AI defaults to bulleted lists for everything
- Human: Use prose when appropriate. Lists only when genuinely useful.

### Vocabulary Patterns

**Puffery and promotional language:**
- "Revolutionary", "groundbreaking", "game-changing", "cutting-edge"
- "Pivotal moment", "broader movement", "transformative"
- Sounds like a TV commercial? Cut it.

**Hedging language:**
- "It's important to note that...", "It's worth mentioning..."
- "This suggests that...", "It could be argued..."
- Just state the thing directly.

**Weasel words:**
- "Many experts believe...", "Studies have shown..."
- "It is widely considered..."
- Be specific or cut it.

**Overused AI vocabulary:**
- "Delve", "foster", "leverage", "synergy", "holistic"
- "Tapestry", "landscape", "nuanced", "multifaceted"
- "Offers opportunities", "provides insights"

**Sycophantic openers:**
- "Great question!", "Absolutely!", "That's a fantastic point!"
- Just answer.

### Construction Patterns

**"Not just X, it's Y":**
- AI: "This isn't just a tool, it's a revolution"
- Human: Pick one claim and make it well

**Inflated importance:**
- Overstating minor details as "pivotal" or "crucial"
- Match emphasis to actual significance

**Generic positivity:**
- AI replaces specific facts with generic praise
- Use concrete details instead

**Servile tone:**
- Overly accommodating, eager to please
- Be confident and direct

## Don't Overcorrect

- Some em dashes are fine (just not every other sentence)
- Occasional rule of three is natural
- Not every list needs to become prose
- Removing AI patterns should not make text cold or flat
- Aim for natural human voice, not stripped-down minimalism

## When Text Is Already Clean

If the text doesn't show obvious AI patterns, say so:
"This reads authentically human. No major AI patterns detected."

## When Text Is Heavily AI-Generated

Flag it: "This has significant AI patterns. I've cleaned it, but you may want to rewrite from scratch. The underlying structure can still feel artificial even after surface fixes."
