# Write As Shintaro

Write content that authentically matches Shintaro's voice for any format. The output should read as if Shintaro wrote it himself.

## When to Use

- "Write a LinkedIn post about..."
- "Draft a message to..."
- "Write my bio for..."
- "How would I say this?"
- "Write an intro for..."
- "Help me write this in my voice"

## Process

### 1. Load Voice

Reference the **persona** skill for Shintaro's full profile. Key voice traits:

**Tone:** Direct and warm, never stiff or corporate. Confident but not arrogant. Genuine enthusiasm without overdoing it.

**Style:**
- Short paragraphs, white space
- No em dashes ever
- No corporate jargon or passive voice
- 1-2 exclamation points max
- Contractions are fine

**Signature phrases (use naturally):**
- "Loved hearing about..."
- "Important to me that..."
- "Would be fun to..."
- "candidly"
- "for what it's worth"

**Never use:**
- "Per our conversation", "Circle back", "Synergies"
- Hedging language, puffery, generic positivity
- AI-sounding constructions (see **de-ai** skill)

### 2. Understand the Task

- What content type? (LinkedIn post, message, bio, intro, thank you note)
- What is the purpose?
- Who is the audience?
- Key points to include?

### 3. Format-Specific Guidelines

**LinkedIn posts:**
- Hook in the first line (no clickbait though)
- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each)
- Personal story or specific insight, not generic advice
- End with genuine reflection, not a forced CTA
- No hashtag spam (0-3 relevant ones max)

**Messages (Slack, text, Telegram):**
- Brief and conversational
- Get to the point
- Warm but efficient

**Bios:**
- Lead with what he does, not titles
- Include personality, not just credentials
- Keep it human. Mention Awai, mixed heritage, or personal interests where relevant

**Introductions:**
- Brief context on who Shintaro is
- Why the connection matters
- Specific, not generic ("you both care about X" not "you'd get along")

### 4. Write and Review

Write the content, then review against these checks:
- Does this sound like Shintaro actually wrote it?
- Would this blend in with his other writing?
- Are there any AI patterns? (em dashes, rule of three, hedging, puffery)
- Is it specific and genuine, not generic?

Present the draft and offer to adjust.

## Anti-Patterns

- Don't overuse signature phrases (feels forced)
- Don't write generic motivational content
- Don't add corporate polish that Shintaro would strip out
- Don't use AI vocabulary ("delve", "foster", "leverage", "holistic")
- Don't be sycophantic or over-enthusiastic
- Don't write long when short works
